## üéì VALUE-BASED PRICING FRAMEWORK

### The Formula:

1. **Quantify Current Problem:**
   - How many hours/week is client spending on this?
   - What's their hourly cost? (salary + benefits / 2080 hours)
   - Annual cost of problem = hours √ó cost √ó 52 weeks

2. **Calculate Your Solution Value:**
   - Hours saved per week
   - Annual value = hours √ó cost √ó 52 weeks
   - Other benefits (faster growth, more leads, better CX, etc.)

3. **Price Based on Value:**
   - Charge 1-3 months worth of annual value
   - Position as ROI-positive from day one
   - Emphasize compounding benefits

### Example:

**Client Situation:**
- Marketing manager spending 15 hours/week on lead generation
- $60/hour effective rate
- Annual cost: 15 hours √ó $60 √ó 52 weeks = **$46,800/year**

**Your Solution:**

- Saves 13 hours/week = **$40,560/year value**

**Your Price:**
- 1-2 months of annual value = $3,380-$6,760

## ‚ùì FAQ

**Q: Won't we lose clients if we raise prices?**
A: You'll lose price-sensitive clients (who are usually the hardest to work with anyway) and gain value-focused clients who are easier to work with and more profitable. Net effect: higher revenue, better clients.

**Q: What if a client compares us to a cheaper competitor?**
A: That's why we focus on value, not price. Show ROI, highlight speed/quality differences, share success stories. Clients who choose based on price alone aren't ideal clients.

**Q: Should we charge the same price everywhere?**
A: No. Regional pricing makes sense - charge premium in UAE/Saudi Arabia, competitive in Europe, value in Asia. Same service, different ability to pay.

**Q: How do we transition existing clients to retainers?**
A: Offer 1 month free to try, emphasize the value of ongoing optimization, show what they're missing without it. Make it easy to say yes.

**Q: What if we don't hit these revenue projections?**
A: These are aggressive but achievable targets. Even hitting 50% of projections means doubling or tripling current revenue. The key is taking action on pricing and retainers.
